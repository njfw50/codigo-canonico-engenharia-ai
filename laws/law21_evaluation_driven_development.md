# Canon XXI: The Doctrine of Evaluation-Driven Development (EDD)
> [!IMPORTANT]


## Ensuring Quality and Compliance in AI-Generated Artifacts

### Premise
With the proliferation of AI-generated code and architectural proposals, traditional testing and validation methods are insufficient to guarantee adherence to the Canonical Protocol. This Canon mandates a shift towards Evaluation-Driven Development (EDD), where automated and continuous evaluation metrics are integrated directly into the development lifecycle to ensure the quality, compliance, and architectural integrity of all AI-generated artifacts.

### Article 21.1 — Continuous Canonical Audit Integration
Every AI-generated output, from code snippets to architectural diagrams, must be subjected to a continuous Canonical Audit. This audit must include automated checks against all relevant Canons (e.g., Layer Segregation, Prohibition of Ornamental Patterns, Digital Security) before integration into the main codebase.

### Article 21.2 — Metric-Driven Compliance
Compliance with the Canonical Protocol must be quantifiable through predefined metrics. These metrics will assess factors such as code complexity, adherence to architectural patterns, security vulnerabilities introduced, and cognitive load imposed on human engineers. AI agents are required to optimize their outputs against these metrics.

### Article 21.3 — Feedback Loop for Agentic Refinement
Evaluation results must form a direct feedback loop to the AI agents responsible for generation. This feedback mechanism enables agents to iteratively refine their models and generation strategies, leading to continuous improvement in the quality and compliance of their outputs.

### Architectural Impact
Establishes a robust framework for maintaining high standards of quality and compliance in AI-driven development, preventing the accumulation of technical debt and ensuring that AI contributions consistently align with the Canonical Protocol.
